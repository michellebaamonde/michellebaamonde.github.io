ClearSky Website:
Creating a mobile-first design for a flight booking service
Project Overview
ROLE
UX/UI Designer, Researcher
TOOLS
Sketch, InVision
DURATION
80 hours within 2 weeks
Note: This was a speculative project for Designlab.
BACKGROUND
With more than half of all online bookings for U.S. travelers being completed on a device or tablet, ClearSky wants to create a responsive website that will allow customers to book travel with multiple airlines.
GOAL
Design a responsive booking website that gives users an elevated and thoughtful flying experience from the flight search and booking process.
PROBLEM
Users are overwhelmed by numerous airlines to choose from and a lack of price transparency from start to finish when booking a flight.
SOLUTION
Design a straightforward online booking experience through the clear presentation of flight information with a refreshing and reliable visual design.
How might we make it easier for users to access the key information they need to book a flight?
Research
GOALS
  • Identify pain points and unmet needs people experience when booking flights online.

  • Learn about people’s motivations for booking flights online.
Sources: Travelport Online Travel Trends 2020, Travelport Mobile Travel Trends 2019
MARKET RESEARCH + COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS
I kicked off my research by looking at trends in the online flight booking space.
  • 89% of travelers use metasearch channels to research travel.
  • 81% of travelers want to know what’s included in fares when choosing an airline.
  • 63% say the ability to add on extras is an important factor when choosing an airline.
  • 70% of travelers would book a multi-ticket itinerary for a cheaper price.
  • 61% of travelers research on sites that suggest travel options based on budget.
  • 79% of travelers find push notifications about offers or sales useful.
  • 61% of travelers 'nearly always' or 'sometimes' use a smartphone app to book flights.
  • 59% of business travelers have used social media channels to book a flight.
I reviewed the websites and booking process of airlines and online travel agencies to get a better sense of design patterns and offerings.

1-ON-1 INTERVIEWS
To better understand online flight booking behavior, I interviewed five participants about an online flight booking experience within the past three months.
  • Users feel there is a lack of transparency regarding pricing and fees from start to finish and are overwhelmed by the large number of airlines to choose from.
  • Want to book a flight that will get them to their destination quickly without layovers, complete the booking process in as few steps as possible, and willing to pay more to depart and land at a more convenient time and airport.
  • Want more clarity about what’s included in their fare, make seating and meal selections before checkout, and quickly access key information such as their passport number.
  • Like the flexibility and convenience of booking with different airlines to get the best deal and exploring new destinations.
  • Look for free weekends to plan trips and communicate back and forth with friends and family in order to confirm group travel plans.
EMPATHY MAP
I mapped out my interview notes to get a sense of how the user thinks and feels, which has an effect on their pain points and goals.

USER PERSONA
The target user I am designing for is Meg, an experienced traveler who enjoys looking for new travel destinations. She balances her busy work schedule with fun group trips. While price is important to her, she is willing to pay more for convenience. Meg is no stranger to booking flights online but appreciates efficiency and transparency from the travel sites she uses.

Test
OBJECTIVES
  • Identify roadblocks that keep user from completing the task easily.

  • Find out if the website is easy for users to navigate.
METHOD
Remote tests via Zoom
HIGH FIDELITY USABILITY TEST
Five participants ranging from 25-55 who have booked a flight within the past 3 months were asked to use the prototype to complete the following task:
You want to book a flight from New York to San Francisco for the first week of August. How would you do this?
All participants completed the task without errors, and they reported that booking a flight on the website was easy and straightforward.
AFFINITY MAP

The successes and pain points confirmed what aspects of the design worked and what needed improvement. I also noted key patterns and insights, which gave me a better understanding of flight booking behavior.

The main discovery from testing was that users wanted further transparency with pricing and airline policies before checkout.

DESIGN REVISIONS
I made edits to several screens and added a new one based on feedback from testing.

NOW PLAYING
INSIGHT
Didn’t notice when the screen changed to “Select Return Date” and tried to exit.

EDIT
Added “Select return date” label above date details to further indicate to the user that they are on a new screen.
FLIGHT SUMMARY
PAIN POINT
Wanted more info about fare and baggage policies before booking.

EDIT
Added details to Flight Summary for more transparency before checkout.
PAYMENT INFO
PAIN POINTS
  • Price discrepancy between search results and flight summary
  • Didn’t notice passenger details before payment info.

EDITS
  • Removed initial tax/fee disclaimer that people skipped over and added disclaimer below the price in each flight card.
  • Altered the passenger details layout to make it more noticeable to the user.
PROCESSING
Added a processing payment animation before the confirmation page to keep users engaged and assure users their flight is being officially booked.
CONFIRMATION
PAIN POINTS
Wanted information about a confirmation email.

EDITS
Added a message informing users they will receive a confirmation email.
Reflection
NEXT STEPS
Conduct a second round of usability tests with the updated prototype and make necessary revisions based on feedback.
WHAT I LEARNED
I learned about the importance of transparency to users when booking a service. During research interviews and usability tests, participants expressed the need for certain information in order to make a decision. I aimed to add transparency in every aspect from UX to UI throughout the design process.
