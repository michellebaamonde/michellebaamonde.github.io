Uncover More App:
Helping New Yorkers discover new experiences
Project Overview
ROLE
UX/UI Designer, Researcher
TOOL
Figma
DURATION
6 weeks
CLIENT

Uncover More is a guide on what to eat, see, do, shop, and stay in New York City, providing information on quick and affordable travel plans through a weekly newsletter and Instagram account with 16K+ followers.

Founded by a native New Yorker, Uncover More is on a mission to create a platform that introduces people to fun and unique experiences.

GOAL
Uncover More is looking to grow by offering its community a convenient way of searching for safe, reliable, and fun activities on the go.
PROBLEM
New Yorkers are looking for memorable experiences in the city, but feel overwhelmed with the number of options and spend too much time researching and planning.
SOLUTION
Design the mobile app to help New Yorkers explore curated recommendations around them in a digestible format.
How can we make it easier for people to discover quality experiences in New York City?
Research
GOALS
  • Discover the resources New Yorkers use to find activities and understand their motivations.

  • Identify pain points and unmet needs of New Yorkers when searching for experiences in NYC.
STAKEHOLDER INSIGHTS
The project began with a kickoff call with the founder to get a better understanding of their vision, target audience, business goals, competitors and constraints. This helped me determine what to focus on when conducting research on how people behave when looking for new activities and how they use recommendations.
COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS
I looked into other travel guides and recommendation services for a better understanding of the industry. It was important to note what they were offering and how they worked to know what to incorporate and what to avoid when designing the app.

1-ON-1 INTERVIEWS
Five participants between the ages of 20 and 30 were interviewed about their experiences with planning activities in NYC. Throughout the interviews, I noted pain points, needs, and motivations that were expressed by participants.
BEHAVIORAL ARCHETYPES
Rather than focusing on a singular persona, I looked at the user’s journey with a product as they might exhibit different behaviors at different times. Through the interviews, I unveiled two archetypes to represent the users I will be designing for.

Define
PROBLEM
Users feel overwhelmed by too many options and spend too much time researching, so how can we make it easier for people to discover quality experiences in New York City?
SOLUTION
Provide curated recommendations of NYC experiences around users in an easily digestible format from a trusted resource.
IDEATION

The goal was to take what the founder already offered with her weekly newsletter and social media and deliver it to a mobile app user via an interactive, pocket-sized guide. She built a large community by providing high quality recommendations, descriptions, and tips, which needed to be a key part of the app.

Ideas included detailed activity pages with key information (hours, price, location, photos), descriptions written by the native New Yorker founder, and the ability to browse nearby experiences in real-time through a map.

USER FLOW
I combined the ideas with steps a user would take to find an activity. The user is either looking for an activity spontaneously or planning ahead for a later time.

Design
SKETCHES

Based off the user flow, I listed out the app screens and content for each screen.

It was important to start the user journey with a map view to provide recommendations in real-time.

I referred to popular apps like Google Maps and Yelp to ensure I incorporated familiar design patterns.

HOME: VARIATION 1
Full-screen map encourages the user to interact and find activities right away
HOME: VARIATION 2
Welcome text + search bar below map guides user to search in a specific neighborhood
HOME: VARIATION 3
Welcome text + search bar above map guides user to search in a specific neighborhood
RECOMMENDATIONS
  • Based on selected neighborhood
  • Avoid too many options to prevent overwhelming users
  • Incorporate Eat, See, and Do categories the brand already uses for consistency
FILTERS
Removed screen in initial wireframes after reviewing research:
  • Users usually search for 1-2 people, want a mix of free and priced activities.
  • App focus is on users searching in the moment, so time of day is unnecessary.
ACTIVITY DETAILS
Provide content based on what is important to users when making plans:
  • Photos
  • Key details and tips
  • Booking link, if applicable
  • Complementary recommendations

UI DESIGN

Uncover More had an existing brand color and logo, so my goal was to build upon the original branding with complementary colors, typography, and icons.

Creating a design system was crucial for a smoother design process, and the UI Kit provided developers with easy access to the assets.

The brand is a reliable source that inspires users to try new things and connect with people and businesses, which is what I aimed to evoke with these visual elements.

The light cream and neutrals allow the brand’s navy colors to pop, and the range of colors that represent categories helps the user differentiate between them on the map.

Test
OBJECTIVES
  • Discover unclear elements that prevent users from completing the task easily.
  • Identify what features were relevant and irrelevant to the user in different areas of the app.
METHOD
Remote tests via Zoom
USABILITY TEST
Five participants from 20-30 years old who live in New York City and search for activities regularly were tasked with the following:
You are looking for a fun activity in Harlem to do with your partner/best friend, and you’re willing to splurge. How would you go about doing this?

Users shared their desktop screen and were asked to think aloud as they interacted with the mobile prototype.

After users completed the task, I asked them to interact with the map (if they didn’t interact with it during the task) as well as the Saved and Profile menu items.

AFFINITY MAP
I organized notes from the usability test recordings into successes, pain points, and insights to determine what areas to revise.

Deliver
DESIGN REVISIONS
I worked on edits to address the pain points and insights that came up during testing.
HOME - MAP VIEW
PAIN POINT
Unclear about map icons

INSIGHT
Want to see bookmarks on map

EDITS
  • Increased size to make them more noticeable.
  • Created key to educate users about categories.
  • Added bookmark pin so users can access saved places on the map.
HOME - SPOTLIGHT
Client requested a “Spotlight” banner to highlight a different business each week, a familiar feature from the Uncover More weekly newsletter.
RECOMMENDATIONS
PAIN POINT
Didn't know to swipe left to see more activity cards

EDITS
  • Adjusted spacing to make additional cards per row more visible.
  • Client requested subcategories, so I added a label above the title for each activity card.
FILTERS
Brought back Filters with the addition of subcategories to give users more tailored results.
ACTIVITY DETAILS
PAIN POINT
Wanted to book activity immediately / didn't notice booking information

INSIGHT
Wanted to swipe through more photos

EDITS
  • Brought back "Reserve Tickets" or "Book a Table" button.
  • Added a slider to indicate there are additional photos.
SAVED
Changed list format to match cards with white background so they stand out on beige background.
BOOKMARKS LIST
  • Changed to list view to differentiate it from Recommendations.
  • Users can see more bookmarks at a time.
PROFILE
PAIN POINTS
  • Unclear on how to use the profile (can they interact with friends in the app?)
  • Unclear the referral link is for friends to download the app

EDITS
  • Change "Profile" to "Account"
  • Remove profile photo
  • Change wording to specify what the referral link is for.
HANDOFF
After working with the client to finalize the designs, I prepared a Figma file link that included the following items for the developer:
  • Properties and CSS information for each app screen
  • Notes under each screen for further explanation on how certain features should work
  • Updated prototype for the developer to reference transitions between screens
  • UI Kit containing the app’s UI elements all in one place, including exportable assets

The client followed up a month later to consult with me on adding new Shop and Stay categories. I advised her on which screens would need to be updated accordingly and designed two new pins for the map.

I also offered recommendations when her developers had some questions on the UI for certain flows.

The Uncover More App officially launched in March 2021!
Reflection
This was my first design to product launch. Since I was designing a mobile app from scratch, I could have been pulled into multiple directions. Instead, I focused on having clear communcation between myself, the client, and the users throughout the design process.

This experience was invaluable because I was able to design for a real client with a growing business, and learned how to effectively present my ideas to the client and developers.
